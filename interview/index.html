Notes from a job seeking Software Developer

When I graduated from MIT in 2008, I failed a string of job interviews. I told myself "The people hired over me are not better...they are just good at interviews." This attitude locked the number of job offers firmly at zero. I was naive and arrogant. Eventually I realized that all of my successful classmates spent time to prepare for their interviews. So I solved a few practice problems and landed a job with Microsoft in Redmond.

Fast forward to the summer of 2014. I have just passed 9 out of 10 screening interviews (only failed the first one) and received a handful of amazing offers within three weeks. By sharing my experience, I hope you can acheieve even greater success.
